Labour Reforms

Reform 1

Code on Wages, 2019: The Code on Wages, 2019 aimed to simplify and consolidate the existing labor laws related to wages. It subsumed four existing laws: the Payment of Wages Act, 1936; the Minimum Wages Act, 1948; the Payment of Bonus Act, 1965; and the Equal Remuneration Act, 1976.

Reform 2

Code on Occupational Safety, Health, and Working Conditions, 2020: This code seeks to consolidate and amend the laws regulating the occupational safety, health, and working conditions of the workers. It replaces 13 existing laws.

Reform 3

Code on Social Security, 2020: The Code on Social Security, 2020 aims to consolidate and amend the laws relating to social security with the goal of extending social security to all employees and workers.

Reform 4

Industrial Relations Code, 2020: The Industrial Relations Code seeks to consolidate and amend the laws relating to trade unions, conditions of employment in industrial establishments, and investigation and settlement of industrial disputes.

Reform 5

Fixed-Term Employment: There have been provisions introduced to make it easier for employers to hire workers on a fixed-term basis, providing more flexibility in the labor market.

Reform 6

Ease of Compliance: The government has also emphasized the simplification and digitization of compliance procedures to make it easier for businesses to adhere to labor laws.
